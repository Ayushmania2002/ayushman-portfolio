Decoding Plants at the
Molecular Scale

I am a plant biologist specialising in molecular genetics, molecular dynamics, abiotic stress signalling, and plant–microbe interactions. Currently working as a Senior Research Fellow at the Plant Molecular Physiology Lab, ICAR–IARI, on an ICAR-funded project — “Enhancing Climate Resilience and Ensuring Food Security with Genome Editing Tools” — under the guidance of Dr. Shivani Nagar.

My work sits at the intersection of experimental biology and computation: I run all-atom molecular dynamics simulations of rice signalling proteins under ABA-mediated abiotic stress using GROMACS on HPC clusters (Linux/SLURM), while driving wet-lab goals through Python-based sequencing pipelines, CRISPR editing, metagenomics, and protein–protein interaction assays. The broader aim is to decode the molecular architecture of stress-resilient traits and translate those insights into climate-smart rice for sustainable agriculture in India.

I completed my BS–MS Dual Degree in Biology at IISER Tirupati. My master’s thesis — supervised by Dr. Swarup Roy Choudhury — characterised the transcription factor CYCLOPS in Arachis hypogaea (groundnut) and mapped its downstream interactors in root nodule symbiosis signalling, combining site-directed mutagenesis, RNAi mutant generation in groundnut, molecular docking, and structural modelling.

Molecular Dynamics of the PYL6–ABA–PP2C51 Complex in Oryza sativa

All-atom molecular dynamics simulations of the rice ABA receptor complex PYL6–PP2C51 under abscisic acid (ABA)-mediated abiotic stress, performed using GROMACS on HPC clusters (Linux/SLURM). This work probes the conformational dynamics and binding interfaces of a key stress-signalling gate in rice — providing structural insights to drive climate-adaptive crop improvement and inform genome-editing targets for drought and heat tolerance in India’s staple cereal.

CYCLOPS & Root Nodule Symbiosis in Arachis hypogaea

MS dissertation at IISER Tirupati — characterised the transcription factor CYCLOPS and its downstream interactors in root nodule symbiosis (RNS) signalling in groundnut. Combined site-directed mutagenesis, RNAi mutant generation, hairy root transformation, confocal and electron microscopy, molecular docking, and in-silico structural analyses to map the CCaMK–CYCLOPS signalling axis.

Cis-GS — Cis-regulatory Element Genome Scanner

A standalone desktop application for genome-wide identification and functional characterisation of cis-regulatory elements (CREs) and transcription factor binding sites (TFBS). Integrates a seven-step bioinformatics pipeline — NCBI genome fetching, promoter extraction, IUPAC motif scanning, sequence logo building, expression data integration, co-expression network analysis (Louvain), and K-means temporal clustering — with direct in-app access to PlantTFDB, JASPAR 2024, and HOCOMOCO v11 databases. Full CLI included for server and pipeline use.

🔒 Final stage of production — public release coming soon on GitHub.
